About Addis School

We help kids, families, and teachers find the right video at the right level—without the noise. Our library is organized by grade, subject, content, and sub‑content, so learners jump straight to what matters.

Built to run smoothly on TVs, computers, and touch devices. Arrow‑key navigation and big‑screen focus states make it classroom and living‑room friendly.

Our mission

Give every child a safe, inspiring path to learn through video. We believe in clarity, safety, and access for all learners—at school, at home, and on the go.

How it works

  1. Pick a grade (e.g., 3).
  2. Choose a subject (Science, Math, English, etc.).
  3. Select a content area (e.g., “Energy”).
  4. Open a sub‑content to see curated videos (e.g., “Energy transfers”).

Your selections also help us suggest better results over time.

Safety & curation

We combine manual review with structured metadata to screen out low‑quality or off‑topic videos. Titles, thumbnails, and descriptions are checked for school suitability.

Have a concern about a video? Report it and we’ll take action.

FAQ

Where do the videos come from?

We use public sources (like YouTube) and apply our own filtering, tagging, and review. Only school‑friendly content makes the cut.

Can I suggest a video?

Yes! Use the contact form with the link and the grade/subject it fits.

Does it work on a smart TV?

Absolutely. Navigate with arrow keys and Enter. Focus highlights show where you are.

How do Favorites work?

Mark a video as a favorite. It’s saved under the same grade/subject/content/sub‑content so you can find it fast next time.
